About UOCSF

Building a Safer Digital Future for Every Child

The United Online Child Safety Foundation is a global nonprofit organization dedicated to protecting children from the evolving threats of the digital age. We believe that every child, regardless of where they live, deserves to explore, learn, and connect online without fear of exploitation or harm.

Our Vision

A world where every child can safely access the opportunities of the digital age, free from exploitation, abuse, and harm. We envision technology as a force for good in children's lives, enabling education, connection, and growth while maintaining robust protections.

Our Mission

To protect children worldwide from online exploitation, grooming, AI-enabled abuse, deepfakes, social media harm, and emerging digital threats through comprehensive education, innovative technology, global advocacy, and preventive action.

Our Values

  • Child-Centered: Every decision prioritizes children's safety and wellbeing
  • Evidence-Based: Our programs are grounded in research and data
  • Global & Inclusive: We serve all children regardless of background
  • Collaborative: We partner across sectors for greater impact
  • Transparent: We operate with integrity and accountability

Why UOCSF Exists

The digital landscape is evolving faster than protective measures can keep pace. Children today face threats that didn't exist a decade ago—AI-generated exploitation material, sophisticated grooming tactics across gaming platforms, deepfake harassment, and algorithmic exposure to harmful content.

Traditional approaches to child safety weren't designed for this reality. UOCSF was founded to bridge this gap by bringing together expertise in technology, child development, education, and policy to create comprehensive, adaptive solutions that protect children now and into the future.

We work at the intersection of innovation and protection, ensuring that as technology advances, so do the safeguards that keep children safe.

Our Global Commitment

UOCSF operates with a long-term, global perspective. Online threats don't respect borders, and neither does our response. We collaborate with governments, schools, technology companies, and civil society organizations across more than 150 countries to create a unified approach to child safety.

Our commitment extends to the most vulnerable children—those in underserved communities, conflict zones, and regions with limited digital literacy resources. We believe protection should be universal, not a privilege.

Comprehensive Solutions for Child Safety

Our programs address every dimension of online child protection—from empowering parents with knowledge to developing cutting-edge technology that identifies and prevents threats.

01

Parent & Guardian Education

What It Is

Comprehensive educational programs that equip parents and guardians with the knowledge, skills, and tools to protect their children online. Our curriculum covers platform safety, privacy settings, conversation techniques, warning signs recognition, and incident response.

Who It Helps

Parents, guardians, grandparents, and caregivers of children of all ages—from those managing a toddler's first tablet use to those navigating a teenager's social media presence.

Why It Matters

Parents are the first line of defense in online child safety. Many feel overwhelmed by rapidly changing technology and don't know where to start. Our programs transform uncertainty into confidence, giving families the foundation for ongoing digital safety conversations.

Online Courses Workshops Resource Guides Webinars
02

School & Youth Safety Programs

What It Is

Age-appropriate digital citizenship and safety curriculum for K-12 schools, including lesson plans, interactive activities, educator training, and student-led initiatives. Programs are designed to integrate seamlessly into existing educational frameworks.

Who It Helps

Students from elementary through high school, educators seeking ready-to-use safety curriculum, school administrators implementing comprehensive digital wellness policies, and school counselors supporting affected students.

Why It Matters

Schools are where children spend much of their time and where they can receive consistent, peer-reinforced safety education. By reaching children directly in educational settings, we create shared understanding and empower young people to protect themselves and their peers.

Curriculum Kits Teacher Training Student Workshops Policy Templates

AI-Driven Protection Technology

What It Is

Advanced technological solutions using artificial intelligence and machine learning to detect, prevent, and respond to online threats against children. This includes content analysis systems, behavioral pattern detection, and real-time alert mechanisms provided to platforms and organizations.

Who It Helps

Technology platforms seeking to improve child safety, law enforcement agencies investigating online crimes against children, and organizations requiring advanced threat detection capabilities.

Why It Matters

The scale and speed of online threats require technological solutions. Human moderation alone cannot keep pace with billions of daily interactions. Our AI tools augment human efforts, identifying threats faster and more consistently while continuously learning from new threat patterns.

Understanding the Risks

Digital Threats We Address

Knowledge is the foundation of protection. Understanding these threats—calmly and clearly—empowers families, educators, and communities to recognize warning signs and take preventive action.

Online Grooming

Online grooming is the process by which an adult builds a relationship with a child to gain their trust for the purpose of sexual exploitation or abuse. It can happen across any platform where children communicate.

How It Happens

Groomers often pose as peers or trusted figures. They identify vulnerable children, offer attention and understanding, gradually introduce sexual content, and work to isolate the child from protective adults. This process can take weeks or months.

Where It Occurs

Social media platforms, online games with chat features, messaging apps, forums, and any space where children interact online. Groomers go where children are.

Warning Signs

Secretive online behavior, receiving gifts from unknown sources, inappropriate sexual knowledge, withdrawal from family and friends, and having older online "friends" the child is reluctant to discuss.

Child Exploitation

Child sexual exploitation encompasses the creation, distribution, and possession of abuse imagery, as well as the solicitation or coercion of children into sexual acts online. The digital environment has dramatically expanded the scale and reach of these crimes.

The Scale

Reports of online child exploitation have increased exponentially in recent years. Millions of abuse images circulate online, and behind each image is a real child who has been harmed.

How Technology Is Misused

Encrypted platforms, dark web networks, and sophisticated anonymization techniques are used to share illegal content and evade detection. Perpetrators also use social engineering to obtain images directly from children.

Prevention Focus

We support survivor-centered approaches, work with platforms to improve detection, advocate for stronger legal frameworks, and help develop technologies that can identify victims and remove harmful content.

Deepfakes & AI Misuse

Artificial intelligence is being weaponized to create synthetic child abuse material, deepfake images of real children, and automated systems that facilitate exploitation. This represents one of the fastest-growing threat categories.

AI-Generated Abuse Material

Generative AI can create realistic synthetic imagery of child abuse. While no real child may be depicted, this content fuels demand and normalizes abuse. It also poses challenges for detection systems trained on real imagery.

Deepfake Targeting

Innocent photos of real children—from social media, school websites, or family posts—can be manipulated into explicit content. This is increasingly used for harassment, extortion, and bullying among teens.

Our Response

We're developing detection technologies that identify AI-generated content, advocating for legal frameworks that address synthetic abuse material, and educating families about protecting children's digital footprint.

Social Media & Gaming Risks

The platforms where children spend most of their digital time—social media and online games—present unique safety challenges. From cyberbullying to predatory contact, these everyday spaces require constant vigilance.

Cyberbullying

Persistent harassment, exclusion, impersonation, and public humiliation happen daily on platforms children use. The 24/7 nature of digital spaces means there's no escape, and the psychological impact can be severe.

Predatory Contact

Adults with harmful intent actively use the same platforms as children. Gaming environments, live streaming, and social feeds all provide opportunities for initial contact that can lead to grooming.

Harmful Content

Algorithmic recommendation systems can expose children to age-inappropriate content, self-harm communities, and dangerous challenges. The viral nature of harmful content makes rapid response essential.

Data Privacy & Surveillance

Children's personal data is valuable and vulnerable. From the moment they go online, their information is collected, profiled, and potentially exploited—creating risks that can follow them for life.

Data Collection

Apps, games, and websites routinely collect extensive data from child users—often without meaningful consent or parental awareness. This data includes location, browsing habits, contacts, and personal communications.

Identity Risks

Children's clean credit histories make them attractive targets for identity theft. Stolen child identities can go undetected for years, creating significant problems when the child becomes an adult.

Long-term Impact

The digital footprint created in childhood—including information shared by parents (sharenting)—can affect future opportunities, relationships, and safety. What's shared online often cannot be fully retrieved.
